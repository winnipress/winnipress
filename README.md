# Background

The idea of a Personal Home Page gave birth to PHP, an amazing language to make websites happen in an easy and fun way.  
  
Then the idea of making web-logs with ease was planted into it and today WordPress runs millions of websites around the globe.  
  
WordPress is probably the most beautiful poem written in Human History. And will always be.  
  
## So... why WinniPress?  
  
WinniPress is not here to compete against it, but to walk behind it right into the future.  
  
I really like the WP way of doing things, but want a more minimalistic approach on it.  
  
WinniPress is just a shadow of the WordPress greatness.  
  
And away of the fuss of this fuzzy words I want to say something clear:  
  
> "WordPress is focusing too much on making itself a wittypitty toolbox for crappy websites. It's saying goodbye to developers and walking way too much into nondevs territory. Too many buttons for things that should just be coded. Is code not poetry anymore?"  
  
That being said, WordPress and the new Gutentoy are very cool. Such tools will allow people with a lack of knowledge in the web development field to own or sell a basic site with nicelooking stuff in them.  
  
But, the more it walks into that direction, the more it's walking away from pure web development.  
  
I have always seen WordPress as a PHP framework to make stuff happen. I know it's not. But having fallen in love with an idea that is vanishing release after release, I decided to do something about it.  
  
## So... TF is WinniPress?  
  
> "WinniPress is an opensource PHP based tool to develop webs, which includes an administration area to handle stuff and a framework to create applications in an easy way."  
  
I would say WinniPress is the WordPress essence isolated and cleaned, with a strong focus on developers.  
  
In WinniPress everything flies around the concept of **Post**.  
  
**A Post can be anything**: a recipe, an event, a training plan, a product, a ticket, a movie, a comment, a review, a to-do list or even an article for a personal blog. Whatever.  
  
WinniPress helps handling Posts and offers a *WP way* of creating Custom Post Types and custom functionality to interact with them.  
  
And that's it. WinniPress is not a blogging platform, a text editor or a page builder. It can be those if you want it to be those. But it is not by default and does not force you to look at it as if it was.  
  
### Where are the themes and plugins repository gone?  
  
Removed. You are a developer, and you go seek and find the cool stuff out there in the wild. If you don't find what you need, you create it or pay someone to do so.  Or you stick to the Great WordPress and go press buttons.
  
### Where are comments gone?  
  
The comments system is the first thing I threw away.
  
### More things that are being trashed:  
  
* **Gutenberg**. It's amazing. But HTMLCSSJSBerg is the real boss.  
* **XMLRPC**. Finally.  
* **REST**. GraphQL is cool too. But: Plugin territory.  
* **Emojis**. Seriously?  
* **jQuery**. Thanks for making the web evolve but... you gotta go now.  
* **Multisite**. Too many `if(is_multisite)` around. Plus... low use cases.  
  
## The vision for the future.  
  
Computers are getting more powerful every few months. But really, making thousands of function calls each time a user sees a page cannot be healthy. Let's clean this thing down to make it at least a 77% less bloated.  
  
The vision for WinniPress is to throw away as much as possible and then clean what stays. Focus on security and efficiency. For the rest, whatever makes sense for itself, should become a well-written Plugin.
